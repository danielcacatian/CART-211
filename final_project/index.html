The year
2020

The year is coming to an end, and I am quite sure a lot of us are happy to jump into the next year. Before we get into the new year, however, here is a recap of everything horrible that has happened globally during the year 2020 (the timeline will mostly consist of events that either occurred in Canada/Montreal or will affect Canada).

Review the year

March

  • START OF GLOBAL PANDEMIC

    What begun in China, COVID-19 has made its way to Canada and has been announced as a global pandemic. To contain the virus as best they can, Canada forbid international travels and advised citizens to stay at home. Due to this, some citizens were unable to work and struggled to find sources of income, causing the economy to descend drastically.

    ↓ Read more ↓

    Thankfully, Canada landed a helping hand to those affected by the pandemic by offering stimulus packages such as the Canada Emergency Response Benefit (CESB came later in May). Canada helped ensure that citizens remained financially stable. Graphic designers helped workplaces transition to the online platform, so they can continue working.

April

  • FROM MONTHS TO YEARS

    What was assumed to only last a couple of months ended up lasting longer than we expected. A quarantine extension has been applied by our Prime Minister. It was being extended till the end of summer due to COVID cases being on the rise. Things only started to get worse, and seemed like nothing good was going to come from this situation.

    ↓ Read more ↓

    On the bright side, ever since quarantine began, carbon dioxide emissions decreased substantially due to low energy demands in numerous economic sectors. It may not be a lot, but we've struggled to combat global warming for a while. It's good to know that we were able to make an impact without even trying. Now, imagine if we were trying.

May - June

  • A NEW THREAT; NOT A VIRUS

    The virus was our only concern and the only danger. That's what we thought for a while. Until a man who is supposed to protect us from harm has caused the death of a man named George Floyd in America. Cops have continuously caused the deaths of innocent lives due to racial profiling, and it's sad to see that, throughout the years, society is still racist.

    ↓ Read more ↓

    But after numerous injustices towards black skin-toned individuals, the world said "no more." The Black Lives Matter movement reignited by George Floyd's death and has brought individuals all around the world to help fight against racism. This moment has brought people close together for the greater good and sprung peaceful protests all across the U.S. and Canada.

July - September

  • FROM THE LEAST TO THE MOST

    In Canada, we had one of the least amounts of COVID cases, but from July to September, cases have risen. Provinces across Canada such as Ontario, Quebec and Alberta have gone through a large increase in cases. The reports stayed elevated throughout the months, which led Justin Trudeau to declare that Canada is experiencing its second wave of COVID-19.

    ↓ Read more ↓

    Despite entering the second wave, we've also entered a new era of the internet. 5G has been deployed and is now accessible in America and China providing advanced technologies that 3G and 4G couldn't. Behind the scenes, technology has been achieving milestones during COVID. 5G will launch worldwide in 2020, which makes way to a brighter future for Canada perhaps.

October - November

  • THE FATE OF UNITED STATES

    At the start of October began the presidential elections of the United States, and it started with one of the worse presidential debates. The vote results were looking grim, with Donald Trump being in the lead while Joe Biden was quite behind. It seemed like Donald Trump was going to be in-office again for another four years.

    ↓ Read more ↓

    Thankfully, the votes started to kick in, and the Decomatric Party ended up winning the elections. Joe Biden will now be the new president of the United States. With a new president and political party representing the states, we should expect significant changes for both America and Canada. After everything that's happened, perhaps some good will come from this when we enter into the next year.

2021

    • In conclusion...

    Despite the year 2020 being unpleasant till the end, there was still some good that came out of it. There were a lot more events that happened that I didn't cover, but the sad truth is, they couldn't all be good. The point was to see the silver lining in events that seemed like nothing good could've come from it. 2020 has been a real eye-opener, and as much as I didn't want the year to turn out this way, I believe it needed to, so let's jump into 2021 with utmost optimism!
